Thursday, December 30, 2004 (prefix = st1 /New Delhi):
The international aid agency, Red Cross has warned that the toll  in the tsunami  disaster could go up to 1,00,000. 
The official figures of those confirmed  dead is 80,000. Of these Indonesia accounts for the most at 45,268, followed by Sri Lanka with the toll at 22,493. 
As many as 6,974 people have been killed in India, with 1,829 dead inThailand, 100 inSomalia, 90 inMyanmar, 67 inMaldives, 65 inMalaysia, 10 inTanzania, three inSeychelles, two inBangladeshand one person killed inKenya. 
Rising figures
According to unofficial estimates, the death toll could be over 10,000 in India. 
The hardest hit has been the Andaman and Nicobar islands, with over 3,000 dead. 
Though the official government figure for Car Nicobar is 124, that number could go up dramatically with thousands more still missing. 
In Tamil Nadu, according to official estimates, over 6000 are dead, with 3500 in Nagapattinam alone.
